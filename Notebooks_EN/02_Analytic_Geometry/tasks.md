### Solution

#### Given:
1. \( f(x) = 3x - 1 \)
2. \( g(x) = \sqrt{x} \)

We need to compute \( f(g(x)) \), which means substituting \( g(x) \) into \( f(x) \).

---

#### Steps:
1. Substitute \( g(x) = \sqrt{x} \) into \( f(x) = 3x - 1 \):
   \[
   f(g(x)) = f(\sqrt{x})
   \]

2. Apply the definition of \( f(x) = 3x - 1 \), replacing \( x \) in \( f(x) \) with \( \sqrt{x} \):
   \[
   f(\sqrt{x}) = 3(\sqrt{x}) - 1
   \]

3. Simplify:
   \[
   f(g(x)) = 3\sqrt{x} - 1
   \]

---

### Final Answer:
\[
f(g(x)) = 3\sqrt{x} - 1
\]
