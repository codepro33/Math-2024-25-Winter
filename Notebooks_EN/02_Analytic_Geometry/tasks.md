### Solution

#### Given:
1. $f(x) = 3x - 1$
2. $g(x) = \sqrt{x}$

We need to compute $f(g(x))$, which means substituting $g(x)$ into $f(x)$.

---

![alt text](image.png)

#### Steps:
1. Substitute $g(x) = \sqrt{x}$ into $f(x) = 3x - 1$:
   
   $$ f(g(x)) = f(\sqrt{x}) $$

3. Apply the definition of $f(x) = 3x - 1$, replacing $x$ in $f(x)$ with $\sqrt{x}$:
   
   $$ f(\sqrt{x}) = 3(\sqrt{x}) - 1 $$

5. Simplify:
   
   $$ f(g(x)) = 3\sqrt{x} - 1 $$

---

### Final Answer:

$$ f(g(x)) = 3\sqrt{x} - 1 $$
